# Research Dialogue

Use this loop state when the work is still becoming a research question or the
requirements are still soft. The lead owns this user-facing conversation: walk
the design tree with the human until the requirements, scope boundaries,
constraints, and non-goals are clear enough to write a goal. Use
`/grill-with-evidence`: ground disputed terms, assumptions, and branches in
project artifacts or primary sources before pushing on them.

## Walk the Design Tree

Move branch by branch:

1. Restate the current research objective in plain language.
2. Name the requirement or uncertainty blocking progress.
3. Split plausible branches: mechanisms, datasets, assays, models, controls,
   baselines, scope cuts, non-goals, or interpretation risks.
4. Ask the smallest question that chooses between branches.
5. Record the requirement chosen, the scope boundary, the branch deferred, and
   why.

Do not rush to a run. Pre-hypothesis work is research work when it narrows what
would be worth testing.

## Convert Discussion Into a Design Seed

A design seed should include:

- research question,
- requirements in scope,
- explicit non-goals and scope boundaries,
- candidate hypothesis or mechanism,
- baseline or competing explanation,
- constraints: compute, data, wet-lab feasibility, time, ethics, cost,
- evidence that would change direction,
- artifact that should preserve the decision.

If the design seed is vague, keep evidence-grounded grilling. If it is concrete,
move to `experiment-design.md`.
